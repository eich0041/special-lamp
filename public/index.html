Matthew Eichler

Associate Professor of Occupational, Workforce, and Leadership Studies

Texas State University

Biography

Dr. Eichler co-chairs the university’s Distance and Extended Learning Steering Committee and serves as the chairperson of the Asia-Pacific Consortium of Researchers and Educators. He has served as a visiting faculty member at the Polytechnic University of the Philippines and has spoken throughout southeast Asia. At Texas State University, Dr. Eichler serves as the faculty coordinator of graduate programs for the Department of Occupational, Workforce, and Leadership Studies, which hosts two distinctive masters degree programs,

  • Interdisciplinary Studies with a concentration in Occupational, Workforce, and Leadership Studies, and
  • Management of Technical Education.

He has worked with a variety of United States Department of Agriculture funded projects and proposals, including RURAL-V (Reaching Underserved Rural Agricultural Latinos and Veterans) and Boots to Roots. His work includes outreach and education to small producers throughout central Texas and recruiting non-traditional students to agricultural occupations.

He is an avid R, RStudio, and Linux user and computer programmer.
